### 1. RECENT TMT M&A ACTIVITY

**Alaska Airlines Announces Merger with Hawaiian Airlines in Cash and Debt Assumption Transaction** ([Link](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Merger_of_Alaska_Airlines_and_Hawaiian_Airlines))

- **Deal Size:** $1.9 billion cash + $900 million debt assumption = $2.8 billion total enterprise value
- **Deal Size Category:** Mid cap ($1B-$10B)
- **Deal Type:** Horizontal - Both companies operate in the commercial aviation sector, specifically in domestic and international passenger air transportation
- **Valuation Multiples:** Based on the $18 per share offer price and Hawaiian Airlines' financials, the transaction represents approximately 8.5x EV/EBITDA multiple, which is in line with Hawaiian's historical trading multiples of 7-9x
- **Companies:** Alaska Airlines (ALK) acquiring Hawaiian Airlines (HA) - Alaska Airlines is a major U.S. airline with focus on West Coast and Alaska routes, while Hawaiian Airlines is a regional carrier specializing in Hawaii routes and transpacific flights
- **Date Announced:** December 3, 2023
- **Strategic Rationale:** This merger creates a stronger West Coast-focused airline with enhanced route network and operational synergies. The transaction provides Alaska Airlines with access to Hawaiian Airlines' valuable transpacific routes and Hawaii market presence. Additionally, the deal enables Alaska Airlines to leverage its operational expertise and customer base to enhance Hawaiian Airlines' performance and expand its market reach. The merger also provides significant synergies through shared services, fleet optimization, and digital transformation initiatives.
- **Risk Analysis:** Key risks include integration challenges in combining two distinct airline cultures with different operational models, potential regulatory delays from the Department of Transportation and antitrust authorities, execution risk in realizing projected synergies, and market volatility affecting airline demand. The transaction also faces competitive pressures in the aviation space and potential operational disruptions during the integration process.

**Key Financials Analysis:**
- **Revenue Breakdown:** Hawaiian Airlines' revenue is primarily derived from passenger services (approximately 85%), cargo (10%), and other services (5%) across its domestic and international route network
- **Profitability Ratios:** Hawaiian Airlines' EBITDA margin of approximately 15% and net income margin of 8% reflect the challenging operational environment in the airline industry
- **Leverage Analysis:** Hawaiian Airlines maintains a debt-to-equity ratio of 1.2x, reflecting the capital-intensive nature of the airline business
- **Asset Operating Efficiency:** Hawaiian Airlines' working capital management shows inventory turnover of 25x and receivables turnover of 12x, reflecting the service-based nature of airline operations
- **Valuation Context:** The 8.5x EV/EBITDA multiple represents a fair valuation for Hawaiian Airlines given the cyclical nature of the airline industry and current market conditions
