Overall, trading reflected selective risk-taking rather than a broad market move.Japan’s Nikkei 225 dropped sharply, falling 1.6% to 49,383.29. The decline followed fresh factory data showing manufacturing activity remains under pressure. The S&P Global Flash PMI rose to 49.7 in December from 48.7 in November, still below the 50 level that signals expansion.Markets are closely watching Japan ahead of the Bank of Japan’s policy meeting on Friday. Investors widely expect an interest rate hike. Any shift by the BOJ could ripple through global bond markets, currencies, and even cryptocurrencies.Chinese stocks also moved lower after disappointing November economic figures. Retail sales rose just 1.3% year over year, the slowest pace since 2022. Lending and investment activity weakened as well, raising concerns about growth momentum heading into year-end.Hong Kong’s Hang Seng Index fell 1.5% to 25,235.41.
