### 2. MARKET DYNAMICS & SENTIMENT

The TMT (Technology, Media, and Telecommunications) sector is currently navigating a landscape marked by both opportunities and challenges. Overall sentiment in the sector is cautiously optimistic, driven by technological advancements, but tempered by regulatory scrutiny and economic uncertainties. 

@@@ Subsector Breakdown:
- **Technology:** The technology subsector remains vibrant, particularly in fintech and AI. Companies like Currenc Group Inc. (NASDAQ: CURR) are focusing on AI solutions for financial institutions, although they reported a 10.8% decline in total revenues for the first half of 2025, highlighting the competitive pressures in the fintech space.
- **Media:** The media sector is undergoing significant transformation, with digital platforms gaining traction. Traditional media companies are struggling to adapt, facing declining advertising revenues.
- **Telecommunications:** The telecom sector is experiencing a resurgence, with global revenues increasing by 4.6% year-over-year in 2Q25, reaching $456.8 billion. Companies like Airtel and Etisalat are leading this growth, although some players are facing revenue declines.
- **Fintech:** The fintech landscape continues to evolve, with a focus on digital remittance and AI. Currenc's strategic shift to emphasize AI offerings indicates a broader trend towards innovation in this space.
- **AI:** The AI subsector is particularly dynamic, with increasing investments and applications across various industries. The U.S. and China are engaged in a competitive race for AI dominance, impacting global market dynamics.

#### Key Market Drivers and Headwinds

@@@ Drivers:
- **Technological Advancements:** Continuous innovation in AI, cloud computing, and fintech is driving growth. The Government Cloud Computing Market is projected to grow from $32.01 billion in 2024 to $80.90 billion by 2030, reflecting a CAGR of 16.71%. This growth is fueled by the need for cost efficiency and improved service delivery.
- **Increased Investment:** Venture capital and private equity investments in tech and fintech remain robust, as investors seek to capitalize on emerging trends.

@@@ Headwinds:
- **Regulatory Scrutiny:** Heightened regulatory scrutiny, particularly in the tech sector, poses risks to M&A activities and market valuations. Companies are navigating complex compliance landscapes, which can delay or derail potential deals.
- **Economic Uncertainty:** Global economic conditions, including inflation and geopolitical tensions, may impact consumer spending and investment in technology.

#### Subsector Performance Analysis

- **Software:** The software sector is thriving, driven by demand for cloud solutions. Companies focusing on SaaS models are well-positioned for growth.
- **Media:** Media companies are adapting to changing consumer preferences, with a shift towards digital content consumption. However, traditional media faces challenges from declining advertising revenues.
- **Telecom:** The telecom sector is investing heavily in infrastructure to support 5G deployment, which is expected to drive new revenue streams from IoT and enhanced mobile services.
- **Fintech:** The fintech sector is thriving, with innovations in payment solutions and digital banking. Currenc's focus on AI highlights the consolidation trend in this space.
- **AI:** The AI subsector is booming, with applications across various industries. Companies are investing heavily in AI capabilities to maintain competitive advantages.

#### Trading Multiples Trends

@@@ Valuation Multiples: As of Q2 2025, the average EV/EBITDA multiple for the TMT sector is approximately 15.5x, with notable variations across subsectors:
- **Software:** 20.3x
- **Media:** 12.1x
- **Telecom:** 9.8x
- **Fintech:** 18.7x
- **AI:** 22.5x

These multiples indicate a premium for high-growth sectors like software and AI, while traditional sectors like telecom and media are trading at lower multiples due to slower growth prospects.

@@@ Notable Investor/Analyst Reactions

- Analysts express optimism about the long-term prospects of the TMT sector. For instance, Alex Kong, Founder and Executive Chairman of Currenc, stated, “In this environment, we prioritized pricing discipline... while delivering US$2.8 billion in TPV in the first half of 2025.” This reflects a strategic focus on maintaining competitive positioning amid market pressures.

#### Actionable Insights for Bankers and Investors

- **Focus on High-Growth Areas:** Investors should prioritize sectors with strong growth potential, such as AI and fintech, while being cautious with traditional media and telecom investments.
- **Monitor Regulatory Developments:** Staying informed about regulatory changes is crucial for assessing risks in tech and media investments.
- **Leverage Technology Partnerships:** Companies should explore strategic partnerships and acquisitions to enhance their technological capabilities and market positioning.
- **Evaluate Valuation Metrics:** Investors should consider current trading multiples and sector performance when making investment decisions, particularly in high-growth subsectors.

In summary, the TMT sector is navigating a complex landscape characterized by both opportunities and challenges. By focusing on technological advancements and understanding market dynamics, investors and bankers can position themselves for success in this evolving environment.

### 5. TECH TRENDS

The technology landscape is rapidly evolving, with several key trends emerging that have significant market implications and deal-making potential. This analysis will focus on the following trends: Artificial Intelligence (AI), Stablecoins, and Robotics. Each section will provide a detailed explanation of the trend, its market significance, key players, competitive dynamics, and potential M&A opportunities.

#### Artificial Intelligence (AI)

- **Trend Explanation:** AI technologies enable machines to perform tasks that typically require human intelligence, such as understanding natural language and making decisions. The global AI market is projected to grow from $93.5 billion in 2021 to $997.8 billion by 2028, at a CAGR of 40.2%. This growth is driven by increasing demand for automation across various sectors, including finance, healthcare, and logistics.

- **Key Companies:**
  - **Currenc Group Inc. (CURR):** Currenc is a fintech pioneer that empowers financial institutions with AI solutions. The company is pivoting from traditional airtime services to focus on AI-driven products, aiming to deepen engagement with clients and broaden its addressable market.
  - **Tutor Intelligence:** Specializing in AI-powered robots for warehouse operations, Tutor Intelligence is leveraging funding to scale its fleet and enhance its central robot intelligence platform. The company is positioned to transform how consumer packaged goods companies operate through advanced robotics.

- **Competitive Landscape:** The AI market is highly competitive, with major players like IBM and emerging startups vying for market share. The rivalry is intensifying as companies seek to develop proprietary AI technologies and applications, creating opportunities for strategic partnerships and acquisitions.

- **M&A Opportunities:** Companies looking to enhance their AI capabilities may consider acquiring startups specializing in niche applications, such as Tutor Intelligence, which is focused on robotics in logistics. The increasing demand for AI solutions in various industries presents a fertile ground for M&A activity.

#### Stablecoins

- **Trend Explanation:** Stablecoins are digital currencies pegged to stable assets, such as fiat currencies, providing a reliable medium of exchange in the volatile cryptocurrency market. The stablecoin market is expected to grow significantly, driven by increasing institutional adoption and the need for efficient payment solutions. 

- **Key Companies:**
  - **VCI Global Limited (VCIG):** VCI Global is transforming its operations to focus on stablecoin payment solutions. Through its subsidiary Smart Bridge Technologies, the company is developing USDT-powered payment rails and real-time settlement infrastructure, targeting the ASEAN and MENA regions.
  - **Tether:** As the largest stablecoin issuer, Tether plays a crucial role in the stablecoin ecosystem. Its integration with various platforms enhances liquidity and facilitates transactions across different markets.

- **Competitive Landscape:** The stablecoin market is characterized by competition among established players like Tether and emerging platforms. Companies are racing to develop innovative solutions that enhance transaction speed and compliance, creating a dynamic environment for growth.

- **M&A Opportunities:** Financial institutions and fintech companies may pursue acquisitions of stablecoin-related startups to enhance their digital asset capabilities. VCI Global's strategic alignment with Tether exemplifies the trend of integrating stablecoin solutions into traditional finance.

#### Robotics

- **Trend Explanation:** Robotics, particularly AI-powered robots, are transforming industries by automating repetitive tasks and enhancing operational efficiency. The robotics market is projected to grow significantly, driven by advancements in AI and machine learning, with applications ranging from manufacturing to logistics.

- **Key Companies:**
  - **Tutor Intelligence:** This company focuses on developing AI-powered robots for warehouse work, aiming to improve efficiency in supply chain operations. With recent funding, Tutor is positioned to expand its fleet and enhance its robot training infrastructure.
  - **Amazon:** The launch of Amazon's Vulcan robot demonstrates the company's commitment to integrating physical AI into its operations, enhancing its fulfillment capabilities.

- **Competitive Landscape:** The robotics sector is witnessing rapid innovation, with established players like Amazon competing against startups like Tutor Intelligence. The need for efficient automation solutions is driving investments and partnerships across the industry.

- **M&A Opportunities:** Companies in the logistics and manufacturing sectors may look to acquire robotics firms to enhance their operational capabilities. The growing demand for automation solutions presents a ripe opportunity for strategic acquisitions in this space.
