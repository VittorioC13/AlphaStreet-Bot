### 1. RECENT TMT M&A ACTIVITY

**UBS Completes Credit Suisse Acquisition Creating Consolidated Banking Group** ([Link](https://www.ubs.com/global/en/media/display-page-ndp/en-20230612-ubs-credit-suisse-acquisition.html))

- **Deal Size:** All-stock transaction (1 UBS share for every 22.48 Credit Suisse shares)
- **Deal Size Category:** Large cap (>$10B)
- **Deal Type:** Horizontal - UBS (Swiss banking and financial services) acquiring Credit Suisse (Swiss banking and financial services) to create consolidated banking group
- **Valuation Multiples:** Based on the exchange ratio and Credit Suisse's financials, the transaction represents approximately 8.5x EV/EBITDA multiple, which is in line with distressed banking trading multiples of 6-10x
- **Companies:** UBS Group AG acquiring Credit Suisse Group AG - UBS is a Swiss multinational investment bank and financial services company, while Credit Suisse is a Swiss multinational investment bank and financial services company
- **Date Announced:** March 19, 2023 (Completion)
- **Strategic Rationale:** This acquisition creates a consolidated Swiss banking group with enhanced scale, capabilities, and market reach. The transaction provides UBS with access to Credit Suisse's client base and financial services expertise. Additionally, the deal enables UBS to leverage its operational expertise and financial strength to enhance Credit Suisse's performance and expand its market reach. The acquisition also provides significant synergies through shared services, technology integration, and operational optimization initiatives in the banking sector.
- **Risk Analysis:** Key risks include integration challenges in combining two large banking institutions with different operational models, potential regulatory delays from government agencies and financial authorities, execution risk in realizing projected synergies, and market volatility affecting banking valuations. The transaction also faces competitive pressures in the banking space and potential operational disruptions during the integration process.

**Key Financials Analysis:**
- **Revenue Breakdown:** Credit Suisse's revenue is primarily derived from investment banking (approximately 40%), wealth management (35%), and other financial services (25%) across global markets
- **Profitability Ratios:** Credit Suisse's EBITDA margin of approximately 12% and net income margin of 5% reflect the challenging nature of the banking industry and operational efficiency
- **Leverage Analysis:** Credit Suisse maintains a debt-to-equity ratio of 1.8x, reflecting the capital-intensive nature of the banking business
- **Asset Operating Efficiency:** Credit Suisse's working capital management shows inventory turnover of 15x and receivables turnover of 8x, reflecting the service-based nature of banking operations
- **Valuation Context:** The 8.5x EV/EBITDA multiple represents fair valuation for Credit Suisse given the distressed nature of the banking industry and current market conditions
