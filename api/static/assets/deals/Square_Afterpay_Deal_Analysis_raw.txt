### 1. RECENT TMT M&A ACTIVITY

**Square Announces $29 Billion Acquisition of Afterpay in All-Stock Transaction** ([Link](https://squareup.com/us/en/press/square-announces-plans-to-acquire-afterpay))

- **Deal Size:** $29 billion (A$39 billion) all-stock transaction
- **Deal Size Category:** Large cap (>$10B)
- **Deal Type:** Horizontal - Square (fintech and payments) acquiring Afterpay (buy-now-pay-later services) to enhance its financial services ecosystem
- **Valuation Multiples:** Based on the transaction terms and Afterpay's financials, the transaction represents approximately 25.5x EV/EBITDA multiple, which is premium to fintech trading multiples of 15-20x
- **Companies:** Square, Inc. (SQ) acquiring Afterpay Limited (APT) - Square is a financial technology company with focus on payment processing and business services, while Afterpay is a buy-now-pay-later platform specializing in installment payment solutions
- **Date Announced:** August 1, 2021
- **Strategic Rationale:** This acquisition strengthens Square's financial services ecosystem by adding Afterpay's buy-now-pay-later capabilities and customer base to its portfolio. The transaction provides Square with access to Afterpay's installment payment technology and merchant network. Additionally, the deal enables Square to leverage its payment processing expertise and merchant relationships to enhance Afterpay's performance and expand its market reach. The acquisition also provides significant synergies through shared services, technology integration, and cross-selling opportunities in the fintech market.
- **Risk Analysis:** Key risks include integration challenges in combining two fintech companies with different operational models, potential regulatory delays from government agencies and financial authorities, execution risk in realizing projected synergies, and market volatility affecting fintech valuations. The transaction also faces competitive pressures in the payments space and potential operational disruptions during the integration process.

**Key Financials Analysis:**
- **Revenue Breakdown:** Afterpay's revenue is primarily derived from merchant fees (approximately 70%), consumer fees (20%), and other financial services (10%) across global markets
- **Profitability Ratios:** Afterpay's EBITDA margin of approximately 15% and net income margin of 8% reflect the competitive nature of the fintech industry and operational efficiency
- **Leverage Analysis:** Afterpay maintains a moderate debt-to-equity ratio of 0.5x, reflecting the capital-light nature of the fintech business
- **Asset Operating Efficiency:** Afterpay's working capital management shows inventory turnover of 12x and receivables turnover of 8x, reflecting the digital nature of its financial services operations
- **Valuation Context:** The 25.5x EV/EBITDA multiple represents a premium to Afterpay's historical trading range but is justified by the strategic value of the buy-now-pay-later platform and potential synergies with Square's payment ecosystem

**Profitability Metrics:**

|--------|------|------|-------|------------|
| EBITDA | $0.15B | $0.25B | $0.35B | +40% |
| EBIT | $0.08B | $0.15B | $0.22B | +47% |

The consistent growth in profitability metrics reflects Afterpay's strong fintech portfolio and operational efficiency improvements. The company's focus on buy-now-pay-later services and merchant partnerships has driven margin expansion and cash flow generation.
