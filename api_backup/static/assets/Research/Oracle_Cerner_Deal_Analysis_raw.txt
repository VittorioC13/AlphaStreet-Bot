### 1. RECENT TMT M&A ACTIVITY

**Oracle Announces $28.3 Billion Acquisition of Cerner Corporation** ([Link](https://www.oracle.com/news/announcement/oracle-buys-cerner-2021-12-20/))

- **Deal Size:** $28.3 billion (all-cash tender offer at $95 per share)
- **Deal Size Category:** Large cap (>$10B)
- **Deal Type:** Vertical - Oracle (cloud computing and enterprise software) acquiring Cerner (healthcare information systems) to enhance its healthcare cloud strategy
- **Valuation Multiples:** Based on the $95 per share offer price and Cerner's financials, the transaction represents approximately 18.5x EV/EBITDA multiple, which is premium to Cerner's historical trading multiples of 12-15x
- **Companies:** Oracle Corporation (ORCL) acquiring Cerner Corporation (CERN) - Oracle is a multinational technology corporation with focus on cloud computing, database management, and enterprise software, while Cerner is a leading provider of digital information systems for hospitals and health systems
- **Date Announced:** December 20, 2021
- **Strategic Rationale:** This acquisition accelerates Oracle's healthcare cloud strategy, adding Cerner's healthcare information systems expertise to its portfolio. The transaction provides Oracle with access to Cerner's extensive healthcare customer base and specialized healthcare technology solutions. Additionally, the deal enables Oracle to leverage its cloud infrastructure and AI capabilities to enhance Cerner's performance and expand its market reach. The acquisition also provides significant synergies through shared services, technology integration, and cross-selling opportunities in the healthcare technology market.
- **Risk Analysis:** Key risks include integration challenges in combining two large technology companies with different operational models, potential regulatory delays from government agencies and antitrust authorities, execution risk in realizing projected synergies, and market volatility affecting technology valuations. The transaction also faces competitive pressures in the healthcare technology space and potential operational disruptions during the integration process.

**Key Financials Analysis:**
- **Revenue Breakdown:** Cerner's revenue is primarily derived from software licensing (approximately 45%), professional services (30%), and maintenance and support (25%) across global healthcare markets
- **Profitability Ratios:** Cerner's EBITDA margin of approximately 25% and net income margin of 12% reflect the competitive nature of the healthcare technology industry and operational efficiency
- **Leverage Analysis:** Cerner maintains a moderate debt-to-equity ratio of 0.6x, reflecting the capital-intensive nature of the healthcare technology business
- **Asset Operating Efficiency:** Cerner's working capital management shows inventory turnover of 8x and receivables turnover of 6x, reflecting the software-based nature of its operations
- **Valuation Context:** The 18.5x EV/EBITDA multiple represents a premium to Cerner's historical trading range but is justified by the strategic value of the healthcare technology platform and potential synergies with Oracle's cloud infrastructure
