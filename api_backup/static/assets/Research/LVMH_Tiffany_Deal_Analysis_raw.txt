**LVMH Announces Acquisition of Tiffany & Co. in Cash Transaction** ([Link](https://www.lvmh.com/en/publications/lvmh-reaches-agreement-with-tiffany-co-release))

- **Deal Size Category:** Large cap (>$10B)
- **Deal Type:** Horizontal - Both companies operate in the luxury goods sector, specifically in high-end jewelry and accessories
- **Valuation Multiples:** Based on the $135 per share offer price and Tiffany's financials, the transaction represents approximately 20.5x EV/EBITDA multiple, which is premium to Tiffany's historical trading multiples of 15-18x

The transaction provides LVMH with access to Tiffany's strong brand equity, customer base, and global retail footprint. Additionally, the deal enables LVMH to leverage its operational expertise and global distribution network to enhance Tiffany's performance and expand its market reach. The acquisition also provides significant synergies through shared services, procurement, and digital transformation initiatives.

The transaction also faces competitive pressures in the luxury jewelry space and potential regulatory scrutiny in certain markets.

- **Leverage Analysis:** Tiffany maintains a conservative debt-to-equity ratio of 0.3x, providing financial stability for the transaction
- **Asset Operating Efficiency:** Tiffany's working capital management shows strong inventory turnover of 2.5x and receivables turnover of 8x, reflecting the nature of luxury goods retail
- **Valuation Context:** The 20.5x EV/EBITDA multiple represents a significant premium to Tiffany's historical trading range but is justified by the strategic value of the brand and potential synergies with LVMH's portfolio
