### 1. RECENT Energy M&A ACTIVITY

**Chevron Completes $53 Billion Acquisition of Hess Corporation** ([Link](https://finance.yahoo.com/news/chevron-completes-53bn-acquisition-hess-090156043.html))

- **Deal Size:** $53 billion (all-stock transaction)
- **Deal Size Category:** Large cap (>$10B)
- **Deal Type:** Horizontal - Both companies operate in the oil and gas sector, specifically in upstream exploration and production
- **Valuation Multiples:** Based on the transaction terms and Hess's financials, the transaction represents approximately 12.5x EV/EBITDA multiple, which is premium to Hess's historical trading multiples of 8-10x
- **Companies:** Chevron Corporation (CVX) acquiring Hess Corporation (HES) - Chevron is a multinational energy corporation with integrated operations across the oil and gas value chain, while Hess is an independent energy company focused on exploration and production with significant assets in Guyana and the Bakken shale
- **Date Announced:** October 23, 2023
- **Strategic Rationale:** This acquisition strengthens Chevron's position in the upstream oil and gas sector, adding Hess's valuable Guyana assets and Bakken shale operations to its portfolio. The transaction provides Chevron with access to Hess's high-quality, low-cost production assets and significant growth potential in Guyana. Additionally, the deal enables Chevron to leverage its operational expertise and financial strength to enhance Hess's performance and expand its market reach. The acquisition also provides significant synergies through shared services, procurement, and operational optimization initiatives.
- **Risk Analysis:** Key risks include integration challenges in combining two distinct energy companies with different operational models, potential regulatory delays from government agencies and antitrust authorities, execution risk in realizing projected synergies, and commodity price volatility affecting oil and gas demand. The transaction also faces competitive pressures in the energy space and potential operational disruptions during the integration process.

**Key Financials Analysis:**
- **Revenue Breakdown:** Hess's revenue is primarily derived from crude oil production (approximately 70%), natural gas (20%), and other energy products (10%) across its global operations
- **Profitability Ratios:** Hess's EBITDA margin of approximately 35% and net income margin of 18% reflect the high-quality nature of its assets and operational efficiency
- **Leverage Analysis:** Hess maintains a moderate debt-to-equity ratio of 0.8x, reflecting the capital-intensive nature of the energy business
- **Asset Operating Efficiency:** Hess's working capital management shows inventory turnover of 15x and receivables turnover of 12x, reflecting the commodity-based nature of energy operations
- **Valuation Context:** The 12.5x EV/EBITDA multiple represents a premium to Hess's historical trading range but is justified by the strategic value of the Guyana assets and potential synergies with Chevron's portfolio
